# SQL Query Documentation: Employee Sales Performance

## 1. Summary

This query evaluates sales representative performance by analyzing revenue generation and customer relationships. It answers the business question: **"How do our sales employees perform in terms of revenue, order volume, and customer base?"** This data is critical for performance reviews, commission calculations, and identifying top performers.

## 2. Tables Involved

| Table | Role |
|-------|------|
| **Employees** | Primary dimension table providing employee identification, names, and job titles |
| **Orders** | Transaction header table linking employees to their sales orders |
| **Order Details** | Fact table containing line-item pricing, quantities, and discounts for revenue calculation |

## 3. Key Logic

- **Joins**: Three-table join connecting employees → orders → order details
- **Filter**: `WHERE o.ShippedDate IS NOT NULL` includes only completed orders
- **Revenue Calculation**: Sums line-item values with discount formula: `Quantity × UnitPrice × (1 - Discount)`
- **Average Ticket**: Divides total revenue by distinct order count to get average order value per employee
- **Aggregation**: Groups by employee (EmployeeID, name, title) to calculate performance metrics
- **Sorting**: Orders by TotalRevenue descending to rank employees by revenue contribution

## 4. Output Columns

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **EmployeeID** | Unique employee identifier for tracking and reference |
| **EmployeeName** | Full name of the employee (FirstName + LastName) |
| **Title** | Job title/role of the employee (e.g., "Sales Representative", "Sales Manager") |
| **TotalOrders** | Count of distinct orders processed by this employee |
| **UniqueCustomers** | Count of distinct customers served by this employee (measures account coverage) |
| **TotalRevenue** | Total net revenue generated by the employee after all discounts |
| **AvgTicket** | Average revenue per order for this employee, rounded to 2 decimal places |

## 5. Business Use Case

**Primary Users:**
- **Sales Managers**: Conduct performance reviews and set targets for sales teams
- **HR/Compensation Teams**: Calculate commissions, bonuses, and performance-based incentives
- **Executive Leadership**: Identify top performers for recognition, promotion, or mentorship roles
- **Sales Operations**: Analyze productivity patterns and optimize territory assignments
- **Training Managers**: Identify employees who may need additional coaching or support

**When to Use:**
- Quarterly and annual performance reviews
- Commission and bonus calculations
- Sales team restructuring or territory planning
- Identifying candidates for promotion or leadership development
- Benchmarking individual performance against team averages

## 6. Assumptions & Limitations

**Assumptions:**
- `ShippedDate IS NOT NULL` accurately represents completed sales (not cancelled orders)
- All orders are correctly assigned to the responsible employee
- Revenue is the primary performance metric (not profit margin or customer satisfaction)
- Employee assignments haven't changed significantly over time

**Limitations:**
- **No time period**: Includes all historical data; doesn't distinguish recent vs. past performance
- **No territory adjustment**: Doesn't account for territory size, market potential, or difficulty
- **No product mix**: High-margin vs. low-margin products not differentiated
- **Team sales**: Orders may involve multiple employees, but only one gets credit
- **Tenure bias**: Longer-tenured employees naturally accumulate more orders and customers
- **No qualitative metrics**: Customer satisfaction, retention, or relationship quality not measured

**Edge Cases:**
- Employees with no shipped orders are excluded entirely
- Employees who left the company still appear if they have historical orders
- Transferred employees may show performance from multiple roles/territories
- Administrative employees with occasional sales will appear in results
- AvgTicket can be skewed by a few very large or very small orders

### 7.3 Manual Validation Queries

#### Sanity Check: Verify Total Revenue Across All Employees

```sql
-- Run this query to verify the sum of employee revenues matches company total
SELECT
    SUM(TotalRevenue) AS CompanyTotalRevenue,
    COUNT(*) AS TotalEmployees,
    AVG(TotalRevenue) AS AvgRevenuePerEmployee,
    MIN(TotalRevenue) AS LowestEmployeeRevenue,
    MAX(TotalRevenue) AS HighestEmployeeRevenue
FROM (
    SELECT
        e.EmployeeID,
        SUM(od.Quantity * od.UnitPrice * (1 - od.Discount)) AS TotalRevenue
    FROM Employees e
    JOIN Orders o ON o.EmployeeID = e.EmployeeID
    JOIN [Order Details] od ON od.OrderID = o.OrderID
    WHERE o.ShippedDate IS NOT NULL
    GROUP BY e.EmployeeID, e.FirstName, e.LastName, e.Title
) AS EmployeeRevenue;

-- Expected Result:
-- CompanyTotalRevenue: ~$1,265,793.00
-- TotalEmployees: 9
-- AvgRevenuePerEmployee: ~$140,643.67
-- LowestEmployeeRevenue: ~$68,792.30 (Steven Buchanan)
-- HighestEmployeeRevenue: ~$232,890.90 (Margaret Peacock)
```

#### Validation: Compare Sales Reps vs Managers

```sql
-- Compare performance metrics between sales reps and management
SELECT
    CASE
        WHEN e.Title LIKE '%Manager%' OR e.Title LIKE '%Vice President%' THEN 'Management'
        ELSE 'Sales Representative'
    END AS EmployeeType,
    COUNT(DISTINCT e.EmployeeID) AS EmployeeCount,
    SUM(TotalOrders) AS TotalOrders,
    SUM(TotalRevenue) AS TotalRevenue,
    AVG(TotalRevenue) AS AvgRevenuePerEmployee,
    AVG(AvgTicket) AS AvgTicketSize,
    AVG(UniqueCustomers) AS AvgCustomersPerEmployee
FROM (
    SELECT
        e.EmployeeID,
        e.Title,
        COUNT(DISTINCT o.OrderID) AS TotalOrders,
        COUNT(DISTINCT o.CustomerID) AS UniqueCustomers,
        SUM(od.Quantity * od.UnitPrice * (1 - od.Discount)) AS TotalRevenue,
        ROUND(SUM(od.Quantity * od.UnitPrice * (1 - od.Discount)) / COUNT(DISTINCT o.OrderID), 2) AS AvgTicket
    FROM Employees e
    JOIN Orders o ON o.EmployeeID = e.EmployeeID
    JOIN [Order Details] od ON od.OrderID = o.OrderID
    WHERE o.ShippedDate IS NOT NULL
    GROUP BY e.EmployeeID, e.FirstName, e.LastName, e.Title
) AS EmployeeMetrics
JOIN Employees e ON e.EmployeeID = EmployeeMetrics.EmployeeID
GROUP BY
    CASE
        WHEN e.Title LIKE '%Manager%' OR e.Title LIKE '%Vice President%' THEN 'Management'
        ELSE 'Sales Representative'
    END;

-- Expected: Management may have higher AvgTicketSize but lower TotalOrders
-- Sales Reps should have higher volume but may vary in ticket size
```

#### Validation: Check Employee Customer Coverage

```sql
-- Analyze which employees serve which customers (territory/account assignment)
SELECT
    e.EmployeeID,
    e.FirstName + ' ' + e.LastName AS EmployeeName,
    COUNT(DISTINCT o.CustomerID) AS UniqueCustomers,
    COUNT(DISTINCT o.OrderID) AS TotalOrders,
    CAST(COUNT(DISTINCT o.OrderID) * 1.0 / COUNT(DISTINCT o.CustomerID) AS DECIMAL(5,2)) AS OrdersPerCustomer,
    STRING_AGG(DISTINCT c.Country, ', ') AS CountriesServed
FROM Employees e
JOIN Orders o ON o.EmployeeID = e.EmployeeID
JOIN Customers c ON c.CustomerID = o.CustomerID
WHERE o.ShippedDate IS NOT NULL
GROUP BY e.EmployeeID, e.FirstName, e.LastName
ORDER BY UniqueCustomers DESC;

-- Expected: Shows customer distribution across employees
-- High OrdersPerCustomer indicates strong customer relationships
```

#### Performance Ranking Query

```sql
-- Create performance tiers for employee evaluation
SELECT
    e.EmployeeID,
    e.FirstName + ' ' + e.LastName AS EmployeeName,
    e.Title,
    TotalRevenue,
    CASE
        WHEN TotalRevenue >= 200000 THEN 'Top Performer (>$200K)'
        WHEN TotalRevenue >= 150000 THEN 'High Performer ($150K-$200K)'
        WHEN TotalRevenue >= 100000 THEN 'Solid Performer ($100K-$150K)'
        ELSE 'Developing (<$100K)'
    END AS PerformanceTier,
    RANK() OVER (ORDER BY TotalRevenue DESC) AS RevenueRank,
    PERCENT_RANK() OVER (ORDER BY TotalRevenue DESC) AS PercentileRank
FROM (
    SELECT
        e.EmployeeID,
        e.FirstName,
        e.LastName,
        e.Title,
        SUM(od.Quantity * od.UnitPrice * (1 - od.Discount)) AS TotalRevenue
    FROM Employees e
    JOIN Orders o ON o.EmployeeID = e.EmployeeID
    JOIN [Order Details] od ON od.OrderID = o.OrderID
    WHERE o.ShippedDate IS NOT NULL
    GROUP BY e.EmployeeID, e.FirstName, e.LastName, e.Title
) AS EmployeeMetrics
JOIN Employees e ON e.EmployeeID = EmployeeMetrics.EmployeeID
ORDER BY TotalRevenue DESC;

-- Use this for performance reviews and bonus calculations
-- PercentileRank shows relative performance (0.0 = top, 1.0 = bottom)
```
