# SQL Query Documentation: Top 10 Customers by Net Revenue

## 1. Summary

This query identifies the top 10 highest-revenue customers in the Northwind database. It answers the business question: **"Who are our most valuable customers by total spending, and what are their ordering patterns?"** The analysis focuses on completed orders only and calculates revenue after applying all discounts.

## 2. Tables Involved

| Table | Role |
|-------|------|
| **Customers** | Primary dimension table providing customer identification and geographic information |
| **Orders** | Transaction header table linking customers to their orders and providing order dates |
| **Order Details** | Fact table containing line-item pricing, quantities, and discount information |

## 3. Key Logic

- **Joins**: Three-table join connecting customers → orders → order details
- **Filter**: `WHERE o.ShippedDate IS NOT NULL` ensures only completed/shipped orders are included
- **Calculation**: Net revenue applies discount at line-item level: `Quantity × UnitPrice × (1 - Discount)`
- **Aggregation**: Groups by customer (CustomerID, CompanyName, Country) to calculate total orders, net revenue, and last order date
- **Limit**: `TOP 10` restricts results to the 10 highest-revenue customers
- **Sorting**: Results ordered by NetRevenue descending to rank customers by value

## 4. Output Columns

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **CustomerID** | Unique 5-character customer identifier (e.g., "ALFKI", "BONAP") |
| **CompanyName** | Full business name of the customer company |
| **Country** | Geographic location of the customer for regional analysis |
| **TotalOrders** | Count of distinct completed orders placed by this customer |
| **NetRevenue** | Total revenue generated by the customer after all discounts, summed across all order line items |
| **LastOrderDate** | Date of the customer's most recent order, useful for recency analysis |

## 5. Business Use Case

**Primary Users:**
- **Account Managers**: Prioritize relationship management and retention efforts for high-value customers
- **Sales Leadership**: Identify key accounts for strategic planning and resource allocation
- **Customer Success Teams**: Proactively engage top customers to ensure satisfaction and prevent churn
- **Marketing Teams**: Design VIP programs, loyalty rewards, or targeted campaigns
- **Finance Teams**: Revenue forecasting and risk assessment (concentration analysis)

**When to Use:**
- Monthly/quarterly account reviews
- Customer segmentation and tiering strategies
- Churn risk assessment for high-value accounts
- Sales territory planning and quota setting
- Identifying candidates for upselling or cross-selling initiatives

## 6. Assumptions & Limitations

**Assumptions:**
- `ShippedDate IS NOT NULL` indicates completed transactions (excludes pending/cancelled orders)
- All discounts are accurately recorded in Order Details
- Customer information (name, country) is current and accurate
- Revenue is the primary metric for "top customer" definition (not profit margin)

**Limitations:**
- **Historical bias**: Includes all-time data; long-term customers may rank higher than recent high-spenders
- **No time window**: Doesn't distinguish between active and dormant customers (use LastOrderDate to assess)
- **No profitability**: High revenue doesn't guarantee high profit (discounts, shipping costs, returns not considered)
- **Currency assumption**: Assumes all transactions in same currency or already converted
- **Snapshot view**: TOP 10 is static; rankings change as new orders are placed

**Edge Cases:**
- Customers with only unshipped orders will be excluded entirely
- Customers with 100% discounted orders will show zero or minimal revenue
- Ties at position 10 may result in arbitrary exclusion of equally-ranked customers
- Merged or acquired companies may appear as separate entities if CustomerID changed

### 7.3 Manual Validation Queries

#### Sanity Check: Verify TOP 10 Total Revenue

```sql
-- Run this query to verify the sum of TOP 10 customer revenues
SELECT
    SUM(NetRevenue) AS Top10TotalRevenue,
    COUNT(*) AS CustomerCount,
    MIN(NetRevenue) AS MinRevenueInTop10,
    MAX(NetRevenue) AS MaxRevenueInTop10
FROM (
    SELECT TOP 10
        c.CustomerID,
        c.CompanyName,
        SUM(od.Quantity * od.UnitPrice * (1 - od.Discount)) AS NetRevenue
    FROM Customers c
    JOIN Orders o ON o.CustomerID = c.CustomerID
    JOIN [Order Details] od ON od.OrderID = o.OrderID
    WHERE o.ShippedDate IS NOT NULL
    GROUP BY c.CustomerID, c.CompanyName, c.Country
    ORDER BY NetRevenue DESC
) AS Top10;

-- Expected Result:
-- Top10TotalRevenue: ~$688,387.00
-- CustomerCount: 10
-- MinRevenueInTop10: ~$43,134.40 (HANAR)
-- MaxRevenueInTop10: ~$115,673.40 (SAVEA)
```

#### Validation: Check Customer #11 (Just Outside TOP 10)

```sql
-- Verify the customer that just missed the TOP 10
SELECT TOP 11
    c.CustomerID,
    c.CompanyName,
    c.Country,
    COUNT(DISTINCT o.OrderID) AS TotalOrders,
    SUM(od.Quantity * od.UnitPrice * (1 - od.Discount)) AS NetRevenue,
    MAX(o.OrderDate) AS LastOrderDate
FROM Customers c
JOIN Orders o ON o.CustomerID = c.CustomerID
JOIN [Order Details] od ON od.OrderID = o.OrderID
WHERE o.ShippedDate IS NOT NULL
GROUP BY c.CustomerID, c.CompanyName, c.Country
ORDER BY NetRevenue DESC;

-- Expected: Position 11 should have NetRevenue < $43,134.40
-- This confirms the TOP 10 cutoff is correct
```

#### Validation: Verify Top Customer Details

```sql
-- Deep dive into SAVEA (top customer) to validate calculations
SELECT
    c.CustomerID,
    c.CompanyName,
    c.Country,
    c.City,
    COUNT(DISTINCT o.OrderID) AS TotalOrders,
    SUM(od.Quantity * od.UnitPrice * (1 - od.Discount)) AS NetRevenue,
    MIN(o.OrderDate) AS FirstOrderDate,
    MAX(o.OrderDate) AS LastOrderDate,
    DATEDIFF(day, MIN(o.OrderDate), MAX(o.OrderDate)) AS CustomerLifespanDays,
    COUNT(DISTINCT p.CategoryID) AS CategoriesPurchased
FROM Customers c
JOIN Orders o ON o.CustomerID = c.CustomerID
JOIN [Order Details] od ON od.OrderID = o.OrderID
JOIN Products p ON p.ProductID = od.ProductID
WHERE o.ShippedDate IS NOT NULL
  AND c.CustomerID = 'SAVEA'
GROUP BY c.CustomerID, c.CompanyName, c.Country, c.City;

-- Expected Result:
-- CustomerID: SAVEA
-- CompanyName: Save-a-lot Markets
-- Country: USA
-- TotalOrders: 31
-- NetRevenue: $115,673.40
-- FirstOrderDate: 1997-08-25
-- LastOrderDate: 1998-05-06
-- CustomerLifespanDays: ~254 days
-- CategoriesPurchased: 8 (all categories)
```

#### Validation: Check for Customers with Unshipped Orders Only

```sql
-- Identify customers who have orders but all are unshipped (should not appear in TOP 10)
SELECT
    c.CustomerID,
    c.CompanyName,
    COUNT(o.OrderID) AS TotalOrders,
    SUM(CASE WHEN o.ShippedDate IS NULL THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS UnshippedOrders,
    SUM(CASE WHEN o.ShippedDate IS NOT NULL THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS ShippedOrders
FROM Customers c
JOIN Orders o ON o.CustomerID = c.CustomerID
GROUP BY c.CustomerID, c.CompanyName
HAVING SUM(CASE WHEN o.ShippedDate IS NOT NULL THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) = 0;

-- Expected: Any customers returned here should NOT appear in the TOP 10 query
-- In Northwind, this typically returns 0 rows (all customers have shipped orders)
```
